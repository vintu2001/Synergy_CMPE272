# Lease Violation Policy

## Purpose
This policy outlines the process for addressing lease violations and protecting the rights of all residents and the property.

## Types of Lease Violations

### Material Violations (immediate action)
- Non-payment of rent
- Illegal activities
- Unauthorized occupants
- Subletting without permission
- Property damage (beyond normal wear)
- Threatening or violent behavior
- Health and safety violations

### Non-Material Violations (warning process)
- Noise disturbances
- Pet violations
- Parking violations
- Unauthorized alterations
- Housekeeping standards
- Guest policy violations
- Amenity misuse

## Violation Response Process

### Minor Violations (First Occurrence)
**Response Time**: Within 24 hours of discovery

1. **Written Notice** delivered via:
   - Email to resident's registered email
   - Physical notice posted on door
   - Certified mail (if no response)

2. **Notice Contents**:
   - Specific violation description
   - Reference to lease section violated
   - Required corrective action
   - Deadline to cure (typically 7-10 days)
   - Consequences of non-compliance

3. **Follow-up**:
   - Property inspection if applicable
   - Resident contact within cure period
   - Documentation of resolution or non-compliance

### Repeated Violations (Same Issue)
**Second Occurrence (within 6 months)**:
- Written warning with $100 fine
- 5-day cure period
- Mandatory meeting with property manager
- Probationary status for 90 days

**Third Occurrence (within 12 months)**:
- Written notice of lease termination
- 30-day notice to vacate
- All fines and fees due immediately
- Security deposit subject to forfeiture

### Serious Violations (Immediate Action Required)

#### Non-Payment of Rent
**Timeline**:
- Day 1: Rent due
- Day 4: Late fee assessed ($75 or 5% of rent)
- Day 6: 3-Day Pay or Quit Notice issued
- Day 10: Eviction filing if not paid
- Day 30+: Court hearing and potential eviction

**Options**:
- Payment plan available if contacted by day 5
- Emergency rental assistance referrals provided
- Partial payment accepted to prevent eviction (management discretion)

#### Unauthorized Occupants
**Response**:
- Immediate 10-day notice to remove unauthorized occupant or vacate
- $200 fine per unauthorized occupant
- All occupants must complete application and screening
- Lease modification required for approved additional occupants

#### Illegal Activities
**Response**:
- Immediate 3-day notice to vacate
- No opportunity to cure
- Law enforcement contacted
- Lease terminated
- Security deposit forfeited
- Possible legal action

#### Property Damage
**Response**:
- Immediate assessment of damage
- Repair cost estimate provided within 48 hours
- 10-day notice to pay for repairs or vacate
- If damage exceeds $2,500: immediate lease termination

### Health and Safety Violations

#### Unit Conditions
- Unsanitary conditions
- Fire hazards
- Pest infestation due to housekeeping
- Hoarding situations
- Blocked exits or safety equipment

**Response**:
- Immediate inspection with 24-hour notice (or emergency if health risk)
- 7-day notice to cure
- Professional cleaning/pest control at resident's expense
- Daily monitoring until resolved
- 30-day notice to vacate if not cured

## Documentation Requirements

### All Violations Must Include:
1. Date and time of violation discovery
2. Detailed description with photos/evidence
3. Lease section reference
4. Previous violations of same type
5. Names of witnesses (if applicable)
6. Actions taken by management
7. Resident communications and responses

### Record Retention
- All violation notices: Permanent resident file
- Photos and evidence: Minimum 3 years
- Communication logs: Duration of tenancy + 3 years
- Court documents: Permanent

## Resident Rights

### Right to Cure
- Residents have right to cure most violations within specified timeframe
- Extensions may be granted for good cause (in writing)
- Proof of cure must be provided to management

### Right to Dispute
- Residents may dispute violations within 5 business days
- Written dispute must include evidence/explanation
- Management will review and respond within 3 business days
- Decision may be appealed to regional manager

### Right to Representation
- Residents may have attorney or representative present in all meetings
- Legal aid referrals provided upon request
- Reasonable accommodation requests honored

## Special Circumstances

### Domestic Violence
- Victims of domestic violence have special lease termination rights
- Police report or protective order required
- 30-day notice to terminate lease without penalty
- Confidentiality maintained

### Military Service
- Active duty military have special SCRA protections
- May terminate lease with 30-day notice and PCS orders
- No early termination fees for qualifying orders

### Reasonable Accommodations
- Residents with disabilities may request reasonable accommodations
- Interactive process followed
- Accommodation may modify enforcement of certain policies
- Documentation from healthcare provider required

## Progressive Discipline

### Warning System
**Level 1** (First offense):
- Verbal or written warning
- Education on policy
- Opportunity to cure
- No fine

**Level 2** (Second offense, 6 months):
- Written warning
- $50-$100 fine
- Mandatory meeting
- Probationary period

**Level 3** (Third offense, 12 months):
- Final written warning
- $150-$250 fine
- 30-day probation
- Lease termination if another violation

**Level 4** (Fourth offense or failure to cure):
- Notice to vacate
- Lease termination
- Eviction proceedings if necessary

## Eviction Process

### Legal Requirements
1. Proper notice served (3, 10, 30, or 60 days depending on cause)
2. Notice period expires
3. Unlawful detainer complaint filed with court
4. Summons and complaint served on resident
5. Court hearing (typically 20-30 days)
6. Judgment and writ of possession (if favorable)
7. Sheriff enforces eviction

### Settlement Options
- Payment plans for rent owed
- Stipulated move-out agreements
- Cash for keys offers (voluntary surrender)
- Mediation services available

## Reapplication After Eviction
- Former residents with evictions typically not approved
- Exceptions may be considered after 3 years with:
  - All debts paid in full
  - Positive rental history since eviction
  - Income 4x the rent
  - Additional security deposit
