# Emergency Response Standard Operating Procedure

## Purpose
Provide staff with clear procedures for responding to various emergency situations.

## Scope
All property staff, security personnel, and on-call maintenance technicians.

## Emergency Types and Response

### Fire Emergency

**Immediate Actions**:
1. **Pull fire alarm** immediately upon discovering fire
2. **Call 911** - Provide address, building, floor, unit number
3. **Evacuate building** following designated routes
4. **Do not use elevators**
5. **Close doors** behind you (do not lock)
6. **Meet at designated assembly point** (parking lot north side)

**Staff Responsibilities**:
- **Front Desk**: Monitor fire panel, direct evacuees, maintain resident list
- **Maintenance**: Ensure fire doors close, check stairwells, assist mobility-impaired
- **Security**: Control access, prevent re-entry, coordinate with fire department
- **Manager**: Contact residents, maintain communication log, file incident report

**Equipment Check** (Daily):
- Fire extinguishers (pressure gauge in green)
- Fire alarm pull stations (not damaged)
- Exit signs illuminated
- Emergency lighting functional
- Fire doors unobstructed

**Do Not**:
- Attempt to fight large fires
- Use elevators
- Re-enter building until cleared by fire department
- Block emergency vehicle access

### Medical Emergency

**Assessment**:
1. Determine if person is conscious and breathing
2. Ask if they need help
3. Do not move injured person unless immediate danger

**Call 911 if**:
- Person unconscious or unresponsive
- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding
- Head, neck, or back injury
- Seizures
- Severe allergic reaction

**While Waiting for EMS**:
- Stay with person, provide reassurance
- Send someone to meet EMS at entrance
- Prepare to give: Name, location, description of emergency, person's condition
- Locate AED if cardiac emergency
- Document incident immediately after

**AED Locations**:
- Front desk
- Fitness center
- Pool area
- 3rd floor common area

### Severe Weather (Tornado, Hurricane)

**Tornado Warning**:
1. Alert all residents via emergency system
2. Direct residents to interior hallways/bathrooms (lowest floor)
3. Stay away from windows
4. Cover head and neck
5. Remain sheltered until all-clear

**Hurricane Preparation** (24-48 hours before):
1. Secure outdoor furniture and equipment
2. Board windows if Category 3+
3. Check emergency supplies
4. Fill bathtubs with water
5. Charge all devices
6. Park vehicles away from trees

### Utility Failure

**Power Outage**:
1. Check breaker panel
2. Confirm outage extent (unit, building, area)
3. Contact utility company
4. Activate emergency generators (common areas)
5. Notify residents with estimated restoration time
6. Check on elderly/disabled residents

**Water Main Break**:
1. Locate and shut off main valve if internal
2. Call utility company and maintenance supervisor
3. Post notices about water service interruption
4. Provide bottled water if outage exceeds 4 hours
5. Document affected units

**Gas Leak**:
1. **Evacuate immediately** - Do not use lights, phones, or create sparks
2. **Call 911 and gas company** from safe distance
3. **Do not re-enter** until cleared by gas company
4. **Ventilate area** once cleared

### Elevator Emergency

**Person Trapped**:
1. Use elevator phone/intercom to communicate
2. Reassure trapped person - they are safe
3. Call elevator service company immediately
4. Call 911 if medical emergency
5. Do not attempt rescue
6. Post "Out of Order" signs on all floors

**Elevator Malfunction**:
1. Tag elevator "Out of Order"
2. Call service company
3. Post notices at all elevator banks
4. Arrange temporary assistance for mobility-impaired residents

### Security Threats

**Active Threat/Violence**:
1. **RUN** - Evacuate if safe to do so
2. **HIDE** - If evacuation not possible, lock doors, barricade, silence phones
3. **FIGHT** - As last resort, be aggressive, use improvised weapons
4. **Call 911** when safe - Provide location, description, weapon type

**Suspicious Person/Activity**:
- Observe from safe distance
- Note description: height, weight, clothing, direction
- Do not confront
- Call 911 if threatening behavior
- Alert security/management

### Hazardous Materials Spill

**Small Spill** (less than 1 gallon):
- Contain with absorbent materials
- Ventilate area
- Dispose per hazmat guidelines
- Document incident

**Large Spill** (more than 1 gallon):
- Evacuate area immediately
- Call 911 and hazmat team
- Prevent spread if safe to do so
- Do not attempt cleanup

## Communication Protocol

### Emergency Notification System:
1. **Level 1** (Information): Email/text
2. **Level 2** (Advisory): Email/text/app push
3. **Level 3** (Warning): All channels + phone calls + door-to-door
4. **Level 4** (Evacuation): All channels + alarm system + PA system

## Post-Emergency Procedures

### Immediate (0-24 hours):
1. Account for all residents and staff
2. Assess damage and safety
3. Document everything (photos, notes, costs)
4. File incident report
5. Contact insurance company
6. Begin temporary repairs

### Short-term (1-7 days):
1. Continue damage assessment
2. Coordinate with adjusters
3. Arrange temporary housing if needed
4. Update residents regularly
5. Begin permanent repairs

### Long-term (7+ days):
1. Complete repairs
2. Review and update emergency procedures
3. Conduct staff debriefing
4. Resident town hall meeting
5. Update emergency plans based on lessons learned

## Training Requirements

### All Staff (Quarterly):
- Fire safety and evacuation procedures
- First aid and CPR/AED
- Emergency notification systems
- Communication protocols

### Annual Drills:
- Fire evacuation (twice per year)
- Tornado shelter (once per year)
- Active threat lockdown (once per year)
- Medical emergency simulation
